Lesson 7: STEAM Challenge 🌊

Design, build, and test your very own Floating Litter Boom Prototype (浮動攔截索原型製作) — just like real ocean engineers do!

Your Goals Your Tasks Word Cards AI Rules

7 Lesson 7 STEAM Challenge

Estimated Time: 75 minutes | Format: Hands-On Build & Test

Your Learning Goals

By the end of this lesson, you will be able to:

  • Compare different solution types: Prevent (預防), Capture (攔截), Treat (處理), Enforce (執法), and Educate (教育).
  • Build a working prototype (原型) of a floating litter boom (攔截索).
  • Test your design fairly, record results, and improve your prototype.

Your Success Checklist

  • ✓My boom floats and stays stable in the water tray.
  • ✓I recorded capture rate, stability, and ease of use in the template.
  • ✓I made ONE improvement and wrote why I made it.
  • ✓I submitted my Test Record and Exit Ticket.

Materials List (材料清單)

Collect these before you start building:

🥤
Straws / Foam飲管 / 泡沫板 — For buoyancy (浮力)
🪢
String / Tape繩子 / 膠帶 — For connecting parts (連接物料)
🕸️
Mesh / Net網格 / 網仔 — To catch small trash (攔截小垃圾)
💧
Tray of Water水盤 — Our "Mini Ocean" (模擬海洋)
📄
Paper Bits碎紙 — Our simulated "Litter" (模擬垃圾)

Need Help?

Boom keeps flipping over?

Try adding a small paperclip as a "weight" (砝碼) near the bottom of the mesh. This lowers the centre of gravity!

Paper bits sliding past the boom?

Try a V-shape or curved boom instead of a straight one. This guides the litter toward the middle.

Writing your improvement reason?

Use this frame: "I changed [part] because I noticed _______, and I predicted this would improve _______."

Safety First (安全至上)

  • 🖐️Dry Hands: Keep hands dry when handling tape — it stays stickier! (保持雙手乾爽)
  • 🧹Spills: Have a towel ready. Clean any water off the floor immediately to avoid slipping.
  • ✂️Scissors: Always cut away from your body and fingers.

Your Tasks

Follow in order
1

Warm-up: Solution Types

5 min

Your Mission: Before building, let's think about why we build litter booms. Can you match each solution type to the correct description?

🛑Prevent 預防Stop pollution before it starts
🕸️Capture 攔截Collect pollution already in water
🧪Treat 處理Clean water using technology
⚖️Enforce 執法Use rules & fines to stop polluters
📢Educate 教育Teach people to make better choices
🌊Our Boom = Capture!Today we focus on this one
2

Build Your Litter Boom 🏗️

20 min

Your Mission: Follow the 4-step build guide below. Use the materials on your desk. Take your time — a well-built boom will float better!

1
Create the float barTape several straws side-by-side to make one long floating bar (長形浮條). Make it at least 30 cm.
2
Attach the mesh netTie or tape the mesh underneath the straws so it hangs down into the water — this is the catching part!
3
Anchor the endsSecure both ends with string so the boom can be anchored (固定) to the side of the tray.
4
Float test! 🎉Carefully place your boom in the water tray. Does it float? (測試浮力) If not, adjust before the next step.
3

Test & Record 🧪

20 min

Your Mission: Scatter paper bits in the tray and let them drift toward your boom. Record your results for each criteria in the template below.

Criteria (評審準則) What to measure (測量什麼？)
📦 Capture Rate
攔截率		 Count how many paper bits were stopped by your boom. Out of 10, how many did it catch?
⚖️ Stability
穩定性		 Does your boom flip over or sink? (Rate: Stable / Wobbly / Sinks)
🧹 Ease of Use
易用性		 Can you easily remove the collected litter from the mesh? (Rate: Easy / OK / Hard)
4

Milestone 7: Improve Your Design ✏️

25 min

Your Mission: Look at your test results. Decide on ONE change you want to make. Re-test and record again. Write why you made this specific change!

What you need to do:
  • Look at your test results. Which criteria had the lowest score?
  • Choose ONE change (e.g., add more straws, change the shape to a V, or lower the mesh deeper).
  • Re-run the test with your new design and record the new results.
  • Write your reason using: "I changed [X] because I noticed [Y], and I predicted this would [Z]."

Pro-Tips for Success (成功小貼士)

  • ⚖️Weighting: If your boom floats too high, clip a small paperclip to the mesh as a weight (砝碼) to help it sink deeper.
  • 🌀Simulate currents: If paper bits stick to the tray sides, give the water a gentle stir to simulate real ocean currents (水流).
  • 📐Try a V-shape: A V-shaped or angled boom guides litter toward the centre — much more effective than a straight line!

How you can use AI today

AI is your engineering assistant, not the engineer. Follow these rules for Lesson 7:

Allowed

You may ask AI for ideas to improve your design — like asking "What shape catches the most trash?" Use the prompts in the AI Research Lab above.

Must Do

After using AI for ideas, you must decide which idea to use yourself and write your own reason why. The thinking must be yours!

Not Allowed

Do not copy AI text directly into your improvement reason. Your written explanation must reflect what you actually observed and tested.

Final Step: Exit Ticket

Great work, engineer! Before putting your materials away, answer these two questions in your Exit Ticket:

  • "My litter boom captured _____ out of 10 paper bits."
  • "The ONE change I made was _____, because I noticed _____."
