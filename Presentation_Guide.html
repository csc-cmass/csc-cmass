Presentation Guide

- How to Present Successfully!

1Title Slide ()

What to Include:

  • Group name ()
  • Unit title: "Clean Seas HK" ()
  • Big, colorful design with pictures
DO

Make it eye-catching and fun!

Use colors, emoji, drawings

DON'T

Too much text on title page

Boring, plain design

2Problem Statement ()

Example:

"Thousands of plastic bags end up in Hong Kong harbours every year, harming sea animals."

Include:

  • 1-2 specific examples ()
  • Where it happens ()
  • Why it matters ()
  • Add a photo or drawing

3Evidence & Data ()

Show Your CER (Claim-Evidence-Reasoning):

Claim: "Marine litter is increasing in Hong Kong waters"

Evidence: "In 2024, Beach Clean-up @ Discovery Bay collected 250kg of plastic waste"

Reasoning: "This shows that pollution is a real, growing problem that affects local beaches"

Visualization Tips:

  • Use a bar chart or pie chart ()
  • Include photos from your research ()
  • Show your data from Lesson 4 (Lesson 4 )

4Your Solution ()

Include:

  • What is it? Name of your solution
  • Why does it work? Explain the science
  • How does it work? Step-by-step process
  • Your prototype test results (from Lesson 7)

Example Format:

Solution: "Community Floating Litter Boom at Typhoon Shelter"

Why: "Because waves carry floating litter to sheltered areas where fish live"

How: "We test-built a boom using recycled materials and captured 80% of floating trash"

DO

Show photos of your prototype

Include test results/data

DON'T

Make promises you can't keep

Forget to explain HOW it works

5Stakeholders ()

Show Different Perspectives:

  • Fishermen: "We worry about lower catch if pollution increases"
  • Tourism Industry: "Dirty beaches lose visitors and income"
  • Environmental NGO: "We support solutions that help animals"
  • Government: "We need cost-effective solutions"
  • ‍‍‍ Local Communities: "We care about our home"

How to present:

Create a simple visual showing who is affected + HOW they are affected

TOTAL 2-3 MIN

Role-Play Guide ()

After presenting, your group will have a 1-2 minute Q&A as stakeholders.

Fisher's Perspective:

Ask: "How will your boom affect our fishing nets?" or "What's the cost to us?"

Environmentalist:

Ask: "Will this harm marine life?" or "What about long-term solutions?"

Government Official:

Ask: "How much will this cost?" or "How long to implement?"

Tourism Manager:

Ask: "Will this make our beaches look bad?" or "Timeline for results?"

Tips for Good Answers:

1. Listen carefully - Make sure you understand the question
2. Take a breath - Think for 3-5 seconds before answering
3. Use evidence - Say "Based on our data..." or "Our prototype showed..."
4. Respect disagreement - Say "That's a fair concern because..."
5. If you don't know - Say "Good question! We need to research that"
